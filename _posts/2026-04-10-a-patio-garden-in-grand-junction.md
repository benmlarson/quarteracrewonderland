This month we're visiting a remarkable patio garden in Grand Junction that proves you don't need a yard to grow serious food.

## The Space

The entire garden fits on a 12x16 foot concrete patio behind a townhome. Every square inch is put to use — raised planter boxes line the perimeter, vertical trellises climb the fence, and a collection of grow bags fills the center.

## What's Growing

In mid-April, the garden is already producing:

- **Lettuce and arugula** in shallow planters along the railing
- **Sugar snap peas** climbing a DIY trellis made from cattle panel
- **Strawberries** in a tiered planter that doubles as a privacy screen
- **Herbs** — rosemary, thyme, and oregano in terra cotta pots that overwinter against the south-facing wall

By summer, tomatoes and peppers will take over the grow bags, and pole beans will join the peas on the trellis.

## Lessons from This Garden

The biggest takeaway: **vertical space is free square footage**. By growing up instead of out, this gardener has tripled their effective growing area. The cattle panel trellis cost about $25 and supports hundreds of pounds of produce over the season.

Water management is key on a patio — everything is in containers, so a drip irrigation system on a timer keeps things consistent without daily hand-watering.
