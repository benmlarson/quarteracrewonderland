April's plant of the month is the sugar snap pea — one of the most rewarding crops you can grow in a Western Slope spring garden.

## Why Sugar Snap Peas?

They're one of the first things you can plant in spring, they handle frost without flinching, and there's nothing like eating them straight off the vine while you're working in the garden.

## Growing Tips

Sugar snap peas prefer cool weather, so get them in the ground as soon as you can work the soil — late March to early April on the Western Slope. They'll reward you with an early harvest before the summer heat kicks in.

- **Direct sow** seeds 1 inch deep, 2 inches apart
- **Provide a trellis** — even "bush" varieties climb better with support
- **Water consistently** but don't overdo it; peas don't like soggy feet
- **Harvest often** to encourage more production

## In the Kitchen

Sugar snap peas are perfect raw, but they're also wonderful in a quick stir-fry with sesame oil and garlic. The whole pod is edible — no shelling required.

Once the heat of June arrives, pea production drops off. Pull the spent vines and replace them with beans or cucumbers to keep that trellis working all season.
