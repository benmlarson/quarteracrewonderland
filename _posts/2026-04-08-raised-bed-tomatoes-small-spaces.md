When your tomato plants start producing faster than you can eat them, it's salsa time. This is my go-to recipe — it comes together in minutes and tastes like summer in a bowl.

## Ingredients

- 4 large ripe tomatoes, diced
- 1/2 white onion, finely chopped
- 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
- 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
- Juice of 1 lime
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- Salt to taste

## Instructions

1. Combine the tomatoes, onion, jalapeño, cilantro, and garlic in a bowl
2. Squeeze the lime juice over everything and add a generous pinch of salt
3. Stir gently and let it sit for at least 15 minutes so the flavors meld
4. Taste and adjust the salt and lime

## Tips

- **Tomato choice matters** — paste tomatoes like San Marzano make a thicker salsa, while slicers like Early Girl give you a juicier result
- For a smoother salsa, pulse everything in a food processor for a few seconds
- This keeps in the fridge for 3-4 days, but it rarely lasts that long

The best part of growing your own tomatoes is having salsa ingredients steps from the kitchen. By August, I'm making a batch of this every few days.
